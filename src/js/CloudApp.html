Apps

In Cloudboost, you create an app each of which has its own AppID, Client Key and the Master Key that you apply to your web app. You master key is a secret which should not be shared in your client apps. Your account on CloudBoost can accommodate multiple apps. This is useful even if you have one application, since you can deploy different versions for test and production.

 

CB.CloudApp

This is your CloudApp object, and it is used to initalize your app with CloudBoost.

 

CloudApp.init( appId, appKey )

Init's the app.

ClientKey and MasterKey are the two keys that are provided for each app your create. If you want a client of your app have access to CloudBoost API, its recommended you only share ClientKey with your app.

We dont recommend init'ing your app with MasterKey unless you install our API and make requests from your server.

Arguments
appId
Application ID with which your app is registered. You can find this on your CloudBoost Dashboard
appKey
Client / Master key Secret Key for your app. You can find this on your CloudBoost Dashboard
Returns

void

CB.CloudApp.init('YOUR APP ID', 'YOUR APP KEY');
